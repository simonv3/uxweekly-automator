April 9th, 2015

 

Hey all!

Last week's most popular link was The Big Mistake Nearly Every Designer Makes, which was more about life balance than design.
Make UX Weekly sustainable by supporting it on Patreon.
Here's this week's links:
 

Old: Bargain Basement Usability Testing


Usability testing sounds like it should be complicated and expensive. Here's some tips on how to do it quickly and easily, without costing a small fortune. Oldie but goodie.
 

Short: A Dao of Web Design at 15


15 years since it's been published, the question was whether the author thought it was still applicable. He thinks so. I agree.
 

Longread: Disney's $1 Billion Bet on a Magical Wristband


A look at Disney's MagicBand, which could be a revealing look at the future of wearables. A lot of it is the hype that excite people about wearables, but actually there. Really cool.
 

Apply: Open Design Now


An ebook on how to do open design. There's a number of very interesting articles and design case studies here. 
 

Learn: Usability 101


An introduction to usability from 2012, by Jakob Nielsen. Broken down into a simple What, Why, How, When and Where, though the Where seems to be a bit stuck on at the end.
